== Auxiliary Verb क्या है? 🤔

भाई, सबसे पहले ये समझो — **Auxiliary Verb वो verb है जो main verb की मदद करता है।** इसीलिए इसे **"Helping Verb"** भी कहते हैं।

बिना auxiliary verb के तुम:

* ❌ Negative sentence नहीं बना सकते
* ❌ Question नहीं बना सकते
* ❌ Tenses properly नहीं use कर सकते
* ❌ Passive voice नहीं बना सकते

=== Example देखो:

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| Sentence | Auxiliary | Role

| I *am* eating.
| am
| Continuous tense बनाया

| She *has* finished.
| has
| Perfect tense बनाया

| *Do* you like tea?
| Do
| Question बनाया

| He *can* swim.
| can
| Ability बताई

| You *should* study.
| should
| Advice दी

| I *don't* smoke.
| don't (do+not)
| Negative बनाया
|===

TIP: Auxiliary verb **अकेला मतलब नहीं देता** — ये हमेशा main verb के साथ काम करता है! (कुछ exceptions हैं जो हम आगे देखेंगे)

---

== दो Types — Primary vs Modal 📊

Auxiliary verbs दो categories में बँटते हैं:

[cols="1,2,2"]
|===
| Type | क्या हैं | Special बात

| **Primary Auxiliaries**
| be, have, do
| ये **tenses बनाने**, negative बनाने, questions बनाने में help करते हैं। ये main verb की तरह भी काम कर सकते हैं!

| **Modal Auxiliaries**
| can, could, may, might, shall, should, will, would, must, ought to, need, dare, used to
| ये **mood/attitude** बताते हैं — ability, permission, possibility, advice, obligation, etc.
|===

IMPORTANT: Primary auxiliaries की **forms बदलती हैं** (am/is/are, have/has/had, do/does/did) लेकिन Modals की **forms नहीं बदलतीं** (कोई -s, -ed, -ing नहीं लगता)!

== Quick Reference — सब एक नज़र में 📋

=== Primary Auxiliaries

[cols="1,2,2,3"]
|===
| Verb | Forms | Hindi Meaning | Example

| **Be**
| am, is, are, was, were, been, being
| होना / रहना
| I *am* going. He *was* sleeping.

| **Have**
| have, has, had
| पास होना / चुका होना
| She *has* eaten. I *have* a car.

| **Do**
| do, does, did
| करना (helping role)
| *Do* you know? I *don't* like it.
|===

=== Modal Auxiliaries

[cols="1,2,3"]
|===
| Modal | Hindi Meaning | Example

| **can**
| सकता हूँ
| I *can* swim.

| **could**
| सकता था / क्या आप...
| *Could* you help me?

| **may**
| शायद / क्या मैं...
| *May* I come in?

| **might**
| हो सकता है (कम chance)
| It *might* rain.

| **shall**
| चलें? / करें?
| *Shall* we go?

| **should**
| चाहिए
| You *should* study.

| **will**
| गा / गी / गे
| I *will* come tomorrow.

| **would**
| ता (conditional) / क्या आप...
| *Would* you like tea?

| **must**
| ज़रूर करना चाहिए
| You *must* wear a helmet.

| **ought to**
| करना चाहिए (moral)
| We *ought to* help the poor.

| **need**
| ज़रूरत है
| You *need not* worry.

| **dare**
| हिम्मत करना
| How *dare* you!

| **used to**
| करता था (past habit)
| I *used to* play cricket.
|===

== Subject-wise Form Selection 🎯

ये सबसे important table है — **कौन सा subject → कौन सी form:**

=== Be — कौन सी form कब?

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past

| I
| **am**
| **was**

| He / She / It / Singular Noun
| **is**
| **was**

| You / We / They / Plural Noun
| **are**
| **were**
|===

* **been** → has/have/had के बाद: _I have **been** waiting._
* **being** → is/am/are/was/were के बाद (passive continuous): _The work is **being** done._

=== Have — कौन सी form कब?

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past

| I / You / We / They / Plural
| **have**
| **had**

| He / She / It / Singular
| **has**
| **had**
|===

=== Do — कौन सी form कब?

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past

| I / You / We / They / Plural
| **do**
| **did**

| He / She / It / Singular
| **does**
| **did**
|===

WARNING: Past (did/had/was/were) में सभी subjects के लिए एक ही form है — except **be** जहाँ was (singular) और were (plural)!

== Confusing Pairs — ये सबसे ज़्यादा Confuse करते हैं 🔀

=== 1. Can vs Could 💪

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Can** | **Could**

| Present ability
| Past ability

| _I **can** speak Hindi._
| _I **could** speak French when I was a child._

| Informal permission
| Polite request

| _**Can** I sit here?_ (casual)
| _**Could** you please help me?_ (polite)

| Strong possibility
| Weak possibility / suggestion

| _It **can** be dangerous._ (हो सकता है)
| _It **could** be true._ (शायद सच हो)
|===

TIP: **Could = Can का polite version + past version** — दोनों context देखो!

=== 2. May vs Might 🤔

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **May** | **Might**

| Formal permission
| ❌ Permission में rarely use होता है

| _**May** I come in?_ (formal)
| _(not typically used for permission)_

| Possibility (50-50 chance)
| Possibility (कम chance)

| _It **may** rain today._ (शायद — 50%)
| _It **might** rain today._ (हो सकता है — 30%)

| Present/Future
| Present/Future (+ Past reported speech)

| _She **may** come._ (आ सकती है)
| _She **might** come._ (शायद आए)
|===

IMPORTANT: Permission माँगने के लिए — **May** formal है, **Can** informal है। _May I...?_ = ज़्यादा respectful।

=== 3. Can vs May — Permission का फ़र्क 🙋

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Can I...?** | **May I...?**

| Informal / Casual
| Formal / Respectful

| Friends, family के साथ
| Teacher, boss, elders के साथ

| _**Can** I use your phone?_ (दोस्त से)
| _**May** I use the washroom?_ (teacher से)

| Ability भी बता सकता है
| सिर्फ permission

| _I **can** run fast._ (ability)
| _You **may** leave now._ (permission ✅)
|===

TIP: School/office में **May I...?** use करो — ज़्यादा polite और professional!

=== 4. Should vs Must 📏

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Should** | **Must**

| Advice / Suggestion (soft)
| Obligation / Compulsion (strong)

| _You **should** exercise daily._ (अच्छा होगा अगर करो)
| _You **must** wear a seatbelt._ (ज़रूरी है — कानून है!)

| ना करो तो कोई बात नहीं
| ना करो तो problem होगी

| _You **should** eat healthy._ (सलाह)
| _You **must** pay your taxes._ (obligation)

| Expectation
| Logical deduction (ज़रूर ऐसा ही है)

| _He **should** be home by now._ (होना चाहिए)
| _He **must** be tired after 12 hours._ (ज़रूर थका होगा)
|===

WARNING: **Must not** = मना है! (prohibition) — _You **must not** cheat._ vs **Should not** = नहीं करना चाहिए (advice) — _You **should not** eat junk food._

=== 5. Will vs Would 🔮

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Will** | **Would**

| Future
| Conditional / Polite

| _I **will** come tomorrow._
| _I **would** come if I had time._

| Promise / Decision
| Polite request / Offer

| _I **will** help you._ (पक्का)
| _**Would** you like some tea?_ (polite offer)

| Prediction
| Past habit (repeated)

| _It **will** rain._ (होगा)
| _We **would** go for walks in the evening._ (जाया करते थे)

| Direct
| Softer / More polite
|===

=== 6. Shall vs Will ⚖️

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Shall** | **Will**

| Formal / Suggestion (I/We)
| Normal future (सब subjects)

| _**Shall** we dance?_ (चलें नाचें?)
| _I **will** dance._ (मैं नाचूँगा)

| Offering help
| Stating intention

| _**Shall** I open the window?_ (खोलूँ?)
| _I **will** open the window._ (खोलूँगा)

| British English ज़्यादा
| Universal — हर जगह use होता है

| सिर्फ I / We के साथ
| सब subjects के साथ
|===

TIP: Modern English में **will** ज़्यादा use होता है। **Shall** सिर्फ suggestions/offers के लिए: _Shall I...? Shall we...?_

=== 7. Have to vs Must — दोनों obligation हैं, पर... 🤷

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Must** | **Have to**

| Internal obligation (खुद feel करो)
| External obligation (बाहर से rule है)

| _I **must** study harder._ (मुझे लगता है)
| _I **have to** wear uniform._ (school का rule है)

| Speaker's opinion / decision
| Rule / Law / Someone else's decision

| _You **must** try this food!_ (मेरा suggestion)
| _We **have to** submit by Friday._ (deadline है)

| कोई past form नहीं → had to use करो
| Past: **had to**

| _(no past form)_
| _I **had to** wait for 2 hours._ (wait करना पड़ा)
|===

IMPORTANT: **Must not** (मना है) ≠ **Don't have to** (ज़रूरी नहीं)! +
_You **must not** smoke here._ (Smoking allowed नहीं!) +
_You **don't have to** come._ (आने की ज़रूरत नहीं — लेकिन आ सकते हो)

=== 8. Used to vs Would — Past Habits 🔄

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Used to** | **Would**

| Past habits + Past states दोनों
| सिर्फ past habits (repeated actions)

| _I **used to** live in Delhi._ ✅ (state)
| _I **would** live in Delhi._ ❌ (state के लिए would नहीं!)

| _I **used to** play cricket._ ✅ (action)
| _I **would** play cricket every Sunday._ ✅ (repeated action)

| अब ऐसा नहीं करता — clear contrast
| Past repeated action — nostalgic feel

| Negative: didn't use to
| Negative: would not / wouldn't
|===

WARNING: **Would** सिर्फ repeated **actions** के लिए काम करता है — **states** (live, know, be, have) के लिए हमेशा **used to** use करो!

=== 9. Need to vs Must 🎯

[cols="1,1"]
|===
| **Need to** | **Must**

| Necessity (ज़रूरत है)
| Strong obligation (ज़रूर करो)

| _I **need to** buy groceries._ (ज़रूरत है)
| _I **must** finish this today._ (ज़रूर करना है)

| Softer / Normal
| Stronger / Urgent

| Negative: don't need to (ज़रूरत नहीं)
| Negative: must not (मना है!)

| _You **don't need to** come._ (आने की ज़रूरत नहीं)
| _You **must not** come._ (आने मत! मना है!)
|===

== Auxiliary Verbs in Tenses — कहाँ कौन सा? 📊

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Tense | Auxiliary Used | Example

| Simple Present (Neg/Q)
| do / does
| *Do* you eat? I *don't* eat.

| Present Continuous
| am / is / are
| I *am* eating.

| Present Perfect
| have / has
| I *have* eaten.

| Present Perfect Continuous
| have/has + been
| I *have been* eating.

| Simple Past (Neg/Q)
| did
| *Did* you eat? I *didn't* eat.

| Past Continuous
| was / were
| I *was* eating.

| Past Perfect
| had
| I *had* eaten.

| Past Perfect Continuous
| had + been
| I *had been* eating.

| Simple Future
| will / shall
| I *will* eat.

| Future Continuous
| will + be
| I *will be* eating.

| Future Perfect
| will + have
| I *will have* eaten.

| Future Perfect Continuous
| will + have + been
| I *will have been* eating.
|===

TIP: ऊपर table को ध्यान से देखो — हर tense में exactly कौन से auxiliaries लगते हैं ये clear हो जाएगा!

== Golden Rules — याद रखो! 💡

[NOTE]
====
1. **Primary Auxiliaries** (be, have, do) → Tenses बनाने, Negative बनाने, Questions बनाने में काम आते हैं।
2. **Modal Auxiliaries** → Mood बताते हैं (ability, permission, obligation, possibility, advice)।
3. **Modals के बाद हमेशा V1** (base form) आता है — कोई -s, -ed, -ing नहीं!
** ❌ He can goes. → ✅ He can **go**.
4. **दो Modals एक साथ नहीं** आ सकते!
** ❌ He will can come. → ✅ He **will be able to** come.
5. **Must not = मना है** ≠ **Don't have to = ज़रूरी नहीं** — ये गलती बहुत होती है!
6. जब doubt हो, **polite version use करो** (could > can, would > will, may > can) — कभी गलत नहीं जाएगा!
====
