== Overview — Modal Verbs क्या हैं?

Modal Verbs वो special auxiliary verbs हैं जो **mood / attitude** बताते हैं — ability (सकता हूँ), permission (सकता हूँ), possibility (शायद), advice (चाहिए), obligation (ज़रूर करो)।

=== Modal Verbs के Golden Rules ⭐

[NOTE]
====
1. **Modal + V1 (base form)** — हमेशा! कोई -s, -ed, -ing नहीं!
** ❌ He can goes. → ✅ He can **go**.
2. **दो modals एक साथ नहीं आ सकते!**
** ❌ He will can come. → ✅ He **will be able to** come.
3. **Modals की form नहीं बदलती** — कोई cans, musts, shoulds नहीं!
4. **Negative**: Modal + **not** + V1 → _I cannot go. You should not worry._
5. **Question**: Modal + Subject + V1? → _Can you swim? Should I wait?_
====

---

== 1. CAN — सकता हूँ 💪

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Ability (क्षमता / योग्यता)
कोई काम कर **सकते** हो।

* मैं तैर सकता हूँ। → _I **can** swim._
* वो 5 languages बोल सकता है। → _He **can** speak 5 languages._
* मैं guitar बजा सकता हूँ। → _I **can** play the guitar._

==== Situation 2: Permission (Informal) — "सकता हूँ क्या?"
दोस्तों, family से permission माँगना।

* क्या मैं तुम्हारा phone use कर सकता हूँ? → _**Can** I use your phone?_
* क्या हम यहाँ बैठ सकते हैं? → _**Can** we sit here?_

==== Situation 3: Possibility (सामान्य)
कोई चीज़ possible है।

* Delhi में winters बहुत ठंडी हो सकती हैं। → _Winters in Delhi **can** be very cold._
* ये road busy हो सकती है। → _This road **can** be busy._

==== Situation 4: Request (Informal)
किसी से कुछ करने को कहना।

* क्या तुम window बंद कर सकते हो? → _**Can** you close the window?_
* क्या तुम मुझे ये pass कर सकते हो? → _**Can** you pass me this?_

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + can + V1
| I can drive.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + cannot (can't) + V1
| She can't cook.

| ❓ Question
| Can + Subject + V1?
| Can you help me?
|===

=== Common Mistakes

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why

| He can speaks English.
| He can *speak* English.
| Modal + V1 (no -s!)

| I can to swim.
| I can swim.
| Can + V1 (no "to"!)

| Can you to help?
| Can you help?
| same — no "to" after modal
|===

== 2. COULD — सकता था / क्या आप कृपया... 🎩

"Could" कई roles play करता है — past ability, polite requests, और possibility!

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Past Ability — "कर सकता था"
बचपन या पहले कुछ कर **सकते** थे।

* मैं बचपन में बहुत तेज़ दौड़ सकता था। → _I **could** run very fast in childhood._
* वो पहले French बोल सकती थी। → _She **could** speak French before._

==== Situation 2: Polite Request — "क्या आप... कृपया"
**Can** से ज़्यादा polite!

* क्या आप कृपया दरवाज़ा बंद कर सकते हैं? → _**Could** you please close the door?_
* क्या आप मुझे रास्ता बता सकते हैं? → _**Could** you tell me the way?_

==== Situation 3: Possibility (Uncertain) — "हो सकता है"
* शायद बारिश हो सकती है। → _It **could** rain._
* वो सच हो सकता है। → _That **could** be true._

==== Situation 4: Suggestion — "कर सकते हो"
* तुम उसे call कर सकते हो। → _You **could** call him._
* हम कल try कर सकते हैं। → _We **could** try tomorrow._

==== Situation 5: Past Permission (Reported)
* Teacher ने कहा हम जा सकते थे। → _The teacher said we **could** leave._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + could + V1
| I could swim when I was 5.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + could not (couldn't) + V1
| She couldn't find her keys.

| ❓ Question
| Could + Subject + V1?
| Could you help me, please?
|===

=== "Could have + V3" — "कर सकता था (पर नहीं किया)" 😔

Past में **ability थी पर action नहीं किया** — regret / missed opportunity!

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| तुम उसे call कर सकते थे (पर नहीं किया)।
| You *could have called* him.

| मैं exam pass कर सकता था (लेकिन नहीं पढ़ा)।
| I *could have passed* the exam.

| वो हमारी help कर सकते थे।
| They *could have helped* us.
|===

TIP: **Could have + V3** = past regret! जो **हो सकता था पर नहीं हुआ**।

== 3. MAY — शायद / क्या मैं... (Formal) 🎓

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Formal Permission — "क्या मैं... कर सकता हूँ?"
Teacher, boss, elders से permission — very polite!

* क्या मैं अंदर आ सकता हूँ? → _**May** I come in?_
* क्या मैं एक question पूछ सकता हूँ? → _**May** I ask a question?_
* आप जा सकते हैं। → _You **may** leave now._

==== Situation 2: Possibility (50-50) — "शायद / हो सकता है"
* शायद बारिश हो। → _It **may** rain._
* शायद वो आज late आए। → _He **may** come late today._
* शायद ये plan काम करे। → _This plan **may** work._

==== Situation 3: Wish / Blessing (Formal)
* भगवान तुम्हें लंबी उम्र दे। → _**May** God bless you with a long life._
* तुम्हारी journey safe हो। → _**May** your journey be safe._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + may + V1
| She may come tomorrow.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + may not + V1
| He may not attend the meeting.

| ❓ Question
| May + Subject + V1?
| May I sit here?
|===

NOTE: **May not** — usually contracted form (mayn't) use **नहीं** होता। पूरा बोलो: _may not_!

---

== 4. MIGHT — शायद (कम chances) 🤷

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Weak Possibility — "हो सकता है (पर pata nahi)"
May से **कम chances** express करता है।

* शायद बारिश हो... (कम chance)। → _It **might** rain._
* शायद वो आए, शायद न आए। → _He **might** come, he **might** not._
* शायद ये idea काम करे। → _This idea **might** work._

==== Situation 2: Hypothetical / Unreal Situations
* अगर मैं lottery जीत जाता, तो Goa में घर ले लेता शायद। → _If I won the lottery, I **might** buy a house in Goa._

==== Situation 3: Polite Suggestion — "शायद आप... कर सकते हैं"
* शायद तुम doctor से मिल लो। → _You **might** want to see a doctor._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + might + V1
| She might visit us this weekend.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + might not + V1
| It might not work.

| ❓ Question
| Might + Subject + V1? (rare!)
| Might I suggest something? (very formal)
|===

=== "May have" vs "Might have" + V3 — Past Possibility 🕰️

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| शायद वो पहुँच गया हो। (50%)
| He *may have reached*.

| शायद उसने message देखा हो। (कम chance)
| She *might have seen* the message.

| शायद बारिश हुई हो कल रात।
| It *may/might have rained* last night.
|===

== 5. SHALL — करें? / चलें? 📜

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Suggestion / Offer (I / We)
किसी चीज़ का suggestion या offer — **सिर्फ I / We** के साथ!

* चलें? → _**Shall** we go?_
* मैं window खोलूँ? → _**Shall** I open the window?_
* हम कहाँ बैठें? → _Where **shall** we sit?_
* चाय बनाऊँ? → _**Shall** I make tea?_

==== Situation 2: Formal Promise / Determination (Rare)
* मैं ज़रूर आऊँगा। → _I **shall** definitely come._ (formal/literary)

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| I/We + shall + V1
| We shall overcome.

| ❌ Negative
| I/We + shall not (shan't) + V1
| I shall not forget. (formal)

| ❓ Question
| Shall + I/We + V1?
| Shall I help you?
|===

TIP: Modern English में **shall** सिर्फ questions/suggestions में use होता है: _Shall I...? Shall we...?_ बाकी जगह **will** use करो!

---

== 6. SHOULD — चाहिए 📝

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Advice / Recommendation — "तुम्हें... करना चाहिए"
* तुम्हें ज़्यादा पानी पीना चाहिए। → _You **should** drink more water._
* उसे doctor से मिलना चाहिए। → _He **should** see a doctor._
* तुम्हें रोज़ exercise करनी चाहिए। → _You **should** exercise daily._

==== Situation 2: Expectation — "होना चाहिए"
* वो अब तक पहुँच गया होगा। → _He **should** be there by now._
* ये काम 2 घंटे में हो जाना चाहिए। → _This work **should** be done in 2 hours._

==== Situation 3: Mild Obligation / Duty
* हमें बड़ों का respect करना चाहिए। → _We **should** respect our elders._
* तुम्हें time पर आना चाहिए। → _You **should** come on time._

==== Situation 4: Opinion — "मेरे हिसाब से... करना चाहिए"
* मेरे हिसाब से तुम्हें वो job ले लेनी चाहिए। → _I think you **should** take that job._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + should + V1
| You should study hard.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + should not (shouldn't) + V1
| You shouldn't eat junk food.

| ❓ Question
| Should + Subject + V1?
| Should I wait?
|===

=== "Should have + V3" — "करना चाहिए था (पर नहीं किया)" 😞

Past regret! जो **करना चाहिए था लेकिन नहीं किया!**

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मुझे harder पढ़ना चाहिए था।
| I *should have studied* harder.

| तुम्हें उससे माफ़ी माँगनी चाहिए थी।
| You *should have apologized* to her.

| उसे doctor से मिलना चाहिए था।
| He *should have seen* a doctor.

| हमें ज़्यादा carefully drive करना चाहिए था।
| We *should have driven* more carefully.
|===

WARNING: **Should have + V3** = regret about past! Spoken English में बहुत common — "should've" (should'vuh) बोलते हैं।

== 7. WILL — गा / गी / गे 🚀

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Future Actions
* मैं कल आऊँगा। → _I **will** come tomorrow._
* वो अगले साल शादी करेगा। → _He **will** get married next year._

==== Situation 2: Instant Decision
* Phone बज रहा है — मैं उठाता हूँ। → _The phone is ringing — I **will** get it._
* ठीक है, मैं pizza order करता हूँ। → _Okay, I **will** order pizza._

==== Situation 3: Promise
* मैं promise करता हूँ, late नहीं आऊँगा। → _I promise I **will** not be late._
* मैं तुम्हारी मदद करूँगा। → _I **will** help you._

==== Situation 4: Prediction
* India ये match जीतेगा। → _India **will** win this match._
* कल बारिश होगी शायद। → _It **will** probably rain tomorrow._

==== Situation 5: Offer / Request
* मैं door खोल देता हूँ। → _I'**ll** open the door._
* क्या तुम मेरी help करोगे? → _**Will** you help me?_

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + will + V1
| I will call you later.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + will not (won't) + V1
| She won't come today.

| ❓ Question
| Will + Subject + V1?
| Will you marry me?
|===

TIP: Spoken English में **"I'll"** = I will, **"won't"** = will not — ये contractions बहुत common हैं!

== 8. WOULD — ता (conditional) / polite 🎩

"Would" सबसे versatile modal है — इसके बहुत सारे uses हैं!

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Polite Request / Offer
* क्या आप कृपया window खोल देंगे? → _**Would** you please open the window?_
* क्या आप चाय लेंगे? → _**Would** you like some tea?_
* मैं coffee लूँगा। → _I **would** like a coffee, please._

==== Situation 2: Conditional / Imaginary (अगर... तो)
* अगर मेरे पास पैसे होते, तो मैं Germany जाता। → _If I had money, I **would** go to Germany._
* अगर मैं तुम्हारी जगह होता, तो माफ़ी माँग लेता। → _If I were you, I **would** apologize._

==== Situation 3: Past Habit (Repeated Action)
* हम बचपन में शाम को cricket खेला करते थे। → _We **would** play cricket every evening in childhood._
* दादी हमें कहानियाँ सुनाया करती थीं। → _Grandma **would** tell us stories._

==== Situation 4: Preference (Would rather)
* मैं घर पर रहना पसंद करूँगा। → _I **would rather** stay at home._
* मैं coffee के बजाय चाय पसंद करूँगा। → _I **would rather** have tea than coffee._

==== Situation 5: Future in the Past (Reported Speech)
* उसने कहा कि वो आएगा। → _He said he **would** come._
* मुझे लगा था बारिश होगी। → _I thought it **would** rain._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + would + V1
| I would love to come.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + would not (wouldn't) + V1
| She wouldn't listen.

| ❓ Question
| Would + Subject + V1?
| Would you like some coffee?
|===

=== "Would have + V3" — "होता / करता (पर हुआ नहीं)" 💭

Unreal past! **अगर ऐसा होता तो... लेकिन नहीं हुआ।**

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| अगर बारिश नहीं होती, तो हम picnic गए होते।
| If it hadn't rained, we *would have gone* for a picnic.

| अगर मुझे पता होता, तो मैं ज़रूर आता।
| If I had known, I *would have* definitely *come*.

| अगर तुमने बताया होता, तो मैं help कर देता।
| If you had told me, I *would have helped*.
|===

== 9. MUST — ज़रूर / ज़रूरी है 🔒

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Strong Obligation / Compulsion — "ज़रूर करो!"
* तुम्हें helmet पहनना ज़रूरी है! → _You **must** wear a helmet!_
* हमें rules follow करने चाहिए। → _We **must** follow the rules._
* तुम्हें ये medicine time पर खानी होगी। → _You **must** take this medicine on time._

==== Situation 2: Prohibition — "ये मना है!" (must not)
* यहाँ smoking मना है! → _You **must not** smoke here!_
* ये किसी को मत बताना! → _You **must not** tell anyone!_
* इस area में entry मना है। → _You **must not** enter this area._

==== Situation 3: Logical Deduction — "ज़रूर... होगा"
Evidence-based guess — तुम्हें **strong feel** हो रहा है कि ऐसा ही है।

* वो बहुत थका हुआ होगा (12 घंटे काम किया)। → _He **must** be very tired._ (logical guess)
* कोई घर पर है — lights on हैं। → _Someone **must** be at home — the lights are on._
* ये correct answer ही होगा। → _This **must** be the correct answer._

==== Situation 4: Strong Recommendation — "ज़रूर try करो!"
* तुम्हें ये movie ज़रूर देखनी चाहिए! → _You **must** watch this movie!_
* ये restaurant ज़रूर try करो! → _You **must** try this restaurant!_

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + must + V1
| You must study hard.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + must not (mustn't) + V1
| You mustn't cheat.

| ❓ Question
| Must + Subject + V1? (rare)
| Must I finish today?
|===

=== "Must have + V3" — "ज़रूर... किया होगा" 🕵️

Past logical deduction! **Strong guess about past** — ज़रूर ऐसा ही हुआ होगा।

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| उसने ज़रूर message देखा होगा।
| She *must have seen* the message.

| वो ज़रूर बहुत tired हुआ होगा।
| He *must have been* very tired.

| उन्होंने ज़रूर बहुत practice की होगी।
| They *must have practiced* a lot.

| ज़रूर बारिश हुई होगी — सड़कें गीली हैं।
| It *must have rained* — the roads are wet.
|===

IMPORTANT: **Must not** ≠ **Don't have to** — ये बहुत important है! +
**Must not** = मना है! _You **mustn't** lie._ (झूठ मत बोलो!) +
**Don't have to** = ज़रूरी नहीं। _You **don't have to** come._ (आने की ज़रूरत नहीं — optional)

== 10. OUGHT TO — करना चाहिए (Moral) ⚖️

"Ought to" basically **should** जैसा ही है — बस थोड़ा ज़्यादा formal और moral duty वाला!

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Moral Duty / Right Thing
* हमें गरीबों की मदद करनी चाहिए। → _We **ought to** help the poor._
* तुम्हें अपने parents का respect करना चाहिए। → _You **ought to** respect your parents._

==== Situation 2: Expectation (like should)
* वो अभी तक पहुँच गया होगा। → _He **ought to** have reached by now._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + ought to + V1
| You ought to exercise.

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + ought not to + V1
| You ought not to lie. (formal)

| ❓ Question
| Ought + Subject + to + V1? (very rare)
| Ought I to apologize?
|===

TIP: Daily conversation में **should** use करो — **ought to** formal writing या moral lectures में ज़्यादा suit करता है!

== 11. NEED — ज़रूरत है 🎯

"Need" दो तरह से काम करता है — **Modal** और **Normal Verb**!

=== As Normal Verb (ज़्यादा common)

----
Subject + need/needs + to + V1
Subject + don't/doesn't need + to + V1
Do/Does + Subject + need + to + V1?
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मुझे पानी चाहिए।
| I *need* water. / I *need to* drink water.

| उसे आराम की ज़रूरत है।
| He *needs* to rest.

| तुम्हें यहाँ आने की ज़रूरत नहीं।
| You *don't need to* come here.

| क्या मुझे ये form भरना है?
| *Do* I *need to* fill this form?
|===

=== As Modal Verb (Formal — mainly negative/question)

----
Subject + need not (needn't) + V1
Need + Subject + V1?
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| तुम्हें चिंता करने की ज़रूरत नहीं।
| You *need not (needn't)* worry.

| उसे आने की ज़रूरत नहीं।
| He *need not (needn't)* come.

| क्या मुझे wait करने की ज़रूरत है?
| *Need* I wait?
|===

IMPORTANT: **Need not (needn't)** = ज़रूरी नहीं (no obligation) +
**Must not (mustn't)** = मना है! (prohibition) +
_You **needn't** come._ = आने की ज़रूरत नहीं ✅ +
_You **mustn't** come._ = मत आना! ❌

== 12. DARE — हिम्मत करना 🦁

"Dare" भी dual nature है — Modal और Normal Verb दोनों!

=== As Normal Verb

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| क्या तुम ये करने की हिम्मत रखते हो?
| *Do* you *dare to* do this?

| मैंने उससे बात करने की हिम्मत नहीं की।
| I *didn't dare to* talk to him.

| उसमें सच बोलने की हिम्मत है।
| She *dares to* speak the truth.
|===

=== As Modal Verb (Mainly negative/question — formal)

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| तुम्हारी हिम्मत कैसे हुई??
| How *dare* you!

| उसकी हिम्मत नहीं हुई बोलने की।
| He *dare not (daren't)* speak.

| मुझसे ज़बान मत लड़ाओ!
| Don't you *dare* talk back to me!
|===

TIP: "How dare you!" — ये सबसे famous expression है। Anger / shock express करता है!

== 13. USED TO — करता था (Past Habit) 🔙

"Used to" past की habit बताता है — जो **पहले करते थे, अब नहीं करते!**

=== कब Use करें?

==== Situation 1: Past Regular Action (अब नहीं करते)
* मैं पहले रोज़ cricket खेलता था। → _I **used to** play cricket daily._
* वो पहले यहाँ काम करती थी। → _She **used to** work here._
* हम बचपन में बहुत शरारतें करते थे। → _We **used to** be very naughty in childhood._

==== Situation 2: Past State (अब नहीं है)
* मैं पहले Delhi में रहता था। → _I **used to** live in Delhi._
* वो पहले बहुत shy था। → _He **used to** be very shy._
* यहाँ पहले एक park हुआ करता था। → _There **used to** be a park here._

=== Pattern

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
| Type | Pattern | Example

| ✅ Affirmative
| Subject + used to + V1
| I used to smoke. (अब नहीं)

| ❌ Negative
| Subject + didn't + use to + V1
| I didn't use to like coffee.

| ❓ Question
| Did + Subject + use to + V1?
| Did you use to play sports?
|===

=== तीन अलग-अलग "Used" — Confusion Clear करो! ⚠️

[cols="1,2,3"]
|===
| Expression | Meaning | Example

| **used to + V1**
| पहले करता था (past habit)
| I *used to* smoke. (अब नहीं)

| **be used to + V-ing/Noun**
| आदत है / habitual (present)
| I *am used to* waking up early. (आदत है)

| **get used to + V-ing/Noun**
| आदत पड़ रहा है / adjusting
| I am *getting used to* the new job. (adjust हो रहा हूँ)
|===

WARNING: ये तीनों बहुत confuse करते हैं! +
❌ I am used to wake up early. → ✅ I am used to **waking** up early. +
❌ I used to waking up early. → ✅ I **am** used to waking up early.

== Modal + Have + V3 — Past Modal Summary 📋

ये सबसे important pattern है modals का! **Past** में कुछ बताने के लिए:

[cols="2,3,3"]
|===
| Structure | Meaning | Example

| **could have + V3**
| कर सकता था (पर नहीं किया) — ability
| I *could have helped* you. (पर नहीं किया)

| **should have + V3**
| करना चाहिए था (पर नहीं किया) — regret
| You *should have studied*. (पर नहीं पढ़ा)

| **would have + V3**
| करता / होता (पर हुआ नहीं) — conditional
| I *would have come* if you had called.

| **must have + V3**
| ज़रूर किया होगा — logical deduction
| She *must have left* already.

| **may have + V3**
| शायद किया हो — possibility (50%)
| He *may have seen* the message.

| **might have + V3**
| शायद किया हो — possibility (30%)
| It *might have rained* last night.

| **needn't have + V3**
| करने की ज़रूरत नहीं थी (पर कर दिया)
| You *needn't have bought* this. (ज़रूरत नहीं थी)
|===

IMPORTANT: **Modal + have + V3** = past situation के बारे में बात। ये pattern बहुत powerful है — इसे अच्छे से practice करो!

[%collapsible]
====
1. **May** I come in? _(may — formal permission)_
2. You **should** drink more water. _(should — advice)_
3. He **can** swim. _(can — ability)_
4. It **might** rain tomorrow. _(might — weak possibility)_
5. You **must** wear a helmet! _(must — strong obligation)_
6. I **used to** play guitar in childhood. _(used to — past habit)_
7. **Could** you please close the door? _(could — polite request)_
8. If I had money, I **would** start a business. _(would — conditional)_
9. He **must have seen** my message. _(must have + V3 — logical deduction about past)_
10. I **should have studied** harder. _(should have + V3 — past regret)_
11. You **need not (needn't)** worry. / You **don't need to** worry. _(need — no necessity)_
12. You **must not** smoke here! _(must not — prohibition)_
13. **Shall** we go? Let's eat out. _(shall — suggestion)_
14. How **dare** you! _(dare — shock/anger)_
15. You **could have called** him. _(could have + V3 — missed opportunity)_
16. **Would** you like some tea? _(would — polite offer)_
17. I **am used to** early morning workouts now. _(be used to — habitual present)_
18. I **have to** go to office every day. _(have to — external obligation)_
