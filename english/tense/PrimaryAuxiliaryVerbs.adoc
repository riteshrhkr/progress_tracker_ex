= Primary Auxiliary Verbs — Be, Have, Do 🏗️
:toc: left
:toc-title: Primary Auxiliaries
:toclevels: 3
:icons: font

== Overview

Primary Auxiliary Verbs तीन हैं — **Be**, **Have**, और **Do**। ये तीनों English grammar की **foundation** हैं। इनके बिना तुम tenses, negative sentences, questions — कुछ भी properly नहीं बना सकते।

इनकी **दो roles** हैं:
1. **Main Verb** (अकेले) — _I **am** happy. I **have** a car. I **do** yoga._
2. **Auxiliary / Helping Verb** — _I **am** going. I **have** eaten. **Do** you know?_

IMPORTANT: ये तीनों verbs — be, have, do — English की सबसे ज़्यादा use होने वाली verbs हैं। इन्हें अच्छे से समझ लो तो half grammar clear!

---

== 1. BE — am / is / are / was / were / been / being 🟢

"Be" English का सबसे complex verb है — इसकी **7 forms** हैं! लेकिन tension मत लो, सब systematically समझेंगे।

=== सभी Forms एक नज़र में

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Form | Tense | Example

| **am**
| Present (I)
| I *am* a student.

| **is**
| Present (He/She/It/Singular)
| She *is* happy.

| **are**
| Present (You/We/They/Plural)
| They *are* friends.

| **was**
| Past (I/He/She/It/Singular)
| He *was* tired.

| **were**
| Past (You/We/They/Plural)
| We *were* late.

| **been**
| Perfect forms (has/have/had + been)
| I have *been* waiting.

| **being**
| Continuous Passive (is/was + being + V3)
| The food is *being* cooked.

| **be**
| Base form (after modals, to be)
| You should *be* careful.
|===

---

=== Subject-wise कौन सी Form — Master Table 📋

[cols="2,2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past | Perfect (has/have/had +)

| I
| **am**
| **was**
| been

| He / She / It / Singular
| **is**
| **was**
| been

| You
| **are**
| **were**
| been

| We / They / Plural
| **are**

|===

WARNING: **You** के साथ हमेशा **are / were** — चाहे एक person हो या बहुत! +
❌ You was late. → ✅ You **were** late.

---

=== Role 1: "Be" as Main Verb (मतलब = होना) ⭐

जब "be" अकेला main verb है — तब ये **"होना"**, **"रहना"**, या state/condition बताता है।

==== कब Use करें?

===== Situation 1: Identity / Profession बताना
* मैं एक student हूँ। → _I **am** a student._
* वो एक doctor है। → _She **is** a doctor._
* हम Indians हैं। → _We **are** Indians._

===== Situation 2: Quality / State / Feeling बताना
* मैं खुश हूँ। → _I **am** happy._
* वो थका हुआ था। → _He **was** tired._
* बच्चे excited हैं। → _The children **are** excited._

===== Situation 3: Location बताना
* मैं office में हूँ। → _I **am** at the office._
* किताब table पर है। → _The book **is** on the table._
* वो लोग park में थे। → _They **were** in the park._

===== Situation 4: Age / Size / Color बताना
* मैं 25 साल का हूँ। → _I **am** 25 years old._
* ये room बहुत बड़ा है। → _This room **is** very big._
* आसमान नीला था। → _The sky **was** blue._

==== Pattern — Main Verb के रूप में

===== Affirmative ✅
----
Subject + am/is/are/was/were + Complement (adjective/noun/place)
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मैं ठीक हूँ।
| I am fine.

| वो मेरा दोस्त है।
| He is my friend.

| हम तैयार हैं।
| We are ready.

| कल मौसम अच्छा था।
| The weather was nice yesterday.

| वो लोग बहुत helpful थे।
| They were very helpful.
|===

===== Negative ❌
----
Subject + am/is/are/was/were + not + Complement
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मैं बीमार नहीं हूँ।
| I am not sick.

| वो यहाँ नहीं है।
| He is not (isn't) here.

| हम late नहीं हैं।
| We are not (aren't) late.

| वो खुश नहीं था।
| He was not (wasn't) happy.

| बच्चे घर पर नहीं थे।
| The children were not (weren't) at home.
|===

IMPORTANT: **"Be" main verb होने पर** negative बनाने के लिए **do/does/did की ज़रूरत नहीं** — सीधे not लगाओ! +
❌ I do not am happy. → ✅ I **am not** happy.

===== Interrogative ❓
----
Am/Is/Are/Was/Were + Subject + Complement + ?
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| क्या तुम ठीक हो?
| Are you okay?

| क्या वो teacher है?
| Is she a teacher?

| क्या वो लोग यहाँ थे?
| Were they here?

| तुम कहाँ हो?
| Where are you?

| वो कैसा था?
| How was it?
|===

---

=== Role 2: "Be" as Auxiliary — Continuous Tenses 🔄

"Be" (am/is/are/was/were) + **V1-ing** → Continuous Tenses बनाते हैं।

[cols="2,3,3"]
|===
| Tense | Formula | Example

| Present Continuous
| am/is/are + V1-ing
| I *am studying*. She *is cooking*.

| Past Continuous
| was/were + V1-ing
| He *was sleeping*. They *were playing*.

| Present Perfect Continuous
| have/has + **been** + V1-ing
| I *have been waiting* since 2 PM.

| Past Perfect Continuous
| had + **been** + V1-ing
| She *had been working* for 5 hours.

| Future Continuous
| will + **be** + V1-ing
| I *will be traveling* tomorrow.

| Future Perfect Continuous
| will + have + **been** + V1-ing
| He *will have been studying* for 3 hours.
|===

TIP: देखो — **हर continuous tense** में "be" की कोई न कोई form ज़रूर है! am/is/are → was/were → been → be

---

=== Role 3: "Be" in Passive Voice 🔄

Passive Voice = जब subject काम करने वाला नहीं, बल्कि **काम उस पर हो रहा है।**

----
Subject + be (form) + V3 (Past Participle)
----

[cols="2,3,3"]
|===
| Active (Normal) | Passive | Tense

| She writes a letter.
| A letter *is written* by her.
| Simple Present

| He broke the glass.
| The glass *was broken* by him.
| Simple Past

| They are building a house.
| A house *is being built* by them.
| Present Continuous

| She has cleaned the room.
| The room *has been cleaned* by her.
| Present Perfect

| They will invite us.
| We *will be invited* by them.
| Simple Future
|===

NOTE: Passive voice में **"being"** present/past continuous passive में आता है: _The road **is being** repaired._ +
और **"been"** perfect passive में: _The work **has been** done._

---

=== Common Mistakes with "Be" ⚠️

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Problem क्या है

| I is happy.
| I *am* happy.
| I के साथ am!

| He are a student.
| He *is* a student.
| He/She/It → is

| You was late.
| You *were* late.
| You → always were (past)

| They was playing.
| They *were* playing.
| They → were

| She am cooking.
| She *is* cooking.
| She → is

| I don't am happy.
| I *am not* happy.
| Be main verb → do नहीं लगता!

| He is go to school.
| He *is going* to school.
| Continuous → -ing भूल गए

| The food is cook.
| The food is *being cooked* / *cooked*.
| Passive में V3 + being (continuous)
|===

---

=== Tips & Tricks for "Be" 💡

* 🎯 **"I AM, You ARE, He IS"** — ये mantra याद कर लो, बस!
* 🎯 **Past में: I WAS, You WERE, He WAS, They WERE** — Was (singular), Were (plural + you)
* 🎯 **Contractions** (spoken English में):
** I'm = I am, He's = He is, We're = We are
** Wasn't = Was not, Weren't = Were not, Isn't = Is not, Aren't = Are not
* 🎯 **"Be" + adjective = feeling/state:** _I am tired. She is angry. They were happy._
* 🎯 **"Be" + V-ing = continuous tense:** _I am going. She was cooking._
* 🎯 **"Be" + V3 = passive voice:** _The door is locked. The food was eaten._

== 2. HAVE — have / has / had 🟡

=== सभी Forms एक नज़र में

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Form | Tense/Usage | Example

| **have**
| Present (I/You/We/They)
| I *have* a car. / I *have* eaten.

| **has**
| Present (He/She/It/Singular)
| She *has* a dog. / She *has* finished.

| **had**
| Past (All subjects)
| He *had* a bike. / I *had* eaten.

| **having**
| Continuous (only action meaning)
| I am *having* lunch. (action ✅)

| **have**
| Base form (after modals)
| I will *have* tea. / You should *have* asked.
|===

---

=== Subject-wise कौन सी Form

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past

| I / You / We / They / Plural
| **have**
| **had**

| He / She / It / Singular
| **has**
| **had**
|===

WARNING: Past में सबके लिए **had** — कोई confusion नहीं!

---

=== Role 1: "Have" as Main Verb ⭐

जब "have" अकेला main verb है — तब ये possession, relations, experiences, actions बताता है।

==== कब Use करें?

===== Situation 1: Possession (पास होना)
* मेरे पास एक car है। → _I **have** a car._
* उसके पास दो phones हैं। → _He **has** two phones._
* उनके पास बड़ा घर था। → _They **had** a big house._

===== Situation 2: Relations / Body Parts
* मेरे तीन भाई हैं। → _I **have** three brothers._
* उसकी blue eyes हैं। → _She **has** blue eyes._

===== Situation 3: Illness / Problem
* मुझे सिरदर्द है। → _I **have** a headache._
* उसे बुखार था। → _He **had** a fever._

===== Situation 4: Meals / Experiences (Action meaning)
* चलो चाय पीते हैं। → _Let's **have** tea._
* मैं lunch कर रहा हूँ। → _I am **having** lunch._
* हमने बहुत मज़ा किया। → _We **had** a great time._

===== Situation 5: "Have to" — Obligation (करना पड़ता है)
* मुझे office जाना पड़ता है। → _I **have to** go to the office._
* उसे रोज़ पढ़ना पड़ता है। → _She **has to** study every day._
* मुझे 2 घंटे wait करना पड़ा। → _I **had to** wait for 2 hours._

==== Pattern — Main Verb के रूप में

===== Affirmative ✅
----
Subject + have/has/had + Object
----

===== Negative ❌

NOTE: **"Have" main verb** होने पर negative दो तरीकों से बनता है:

**Method 1 — do/does/did + not + have (ज़्यादा common):**
[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मेरे पास car नहीं है।
| I *do not (don't) have* a car.

| उसके पास time नहीं है।
| She *does not (doesn't) have* time.

| उनके पास ticket नहीं था।
| They *did not (didn't) have* a ticket.
|===

**Method 2 — haven't got (British English):**
[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मेरे पास car नहीं है।
| I *haven't got* a car.

| उसके पास time नहीं है।
| She *hasn't got* time.
|===

===== Interrogative ❓
----
Do/Does/Did + Subject + have + Object + ?
OR
Have/Has + Subject + got + Object + ?
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| क्या तुम्हारे पास pen है?
| Do you have a pen? / Have you got a pen?

| क्या उसके पास laptop है?
| Does he have a laptop? / Has he got a laptop?

| क्या तुम्हें कोई idea था?
| Did you have any idea?
|===

---

=== Role 2: "Have" as Auxiliary — Perfect Tenses ✨

"Have" (have/has/had) + **V3** → Perfect Tenses बनाते हैं।

[cols="2,3,3"]
|===
| Tense | Formula | Example

| Present Perfect
| have/has + V3
| I *have eaten*. She *has gone*.

| Past Perfect
| had + V3
| I *had finished* before he came.

| Future Perfect
| will + have + V3
| I *will have completed* by then.

| Present Perfect Continuous
| have/has + been + V-ing
| I *have been waiting* for 2 hours.

| Past Perfect Continuous
| had + been + V-ing
| She *had been working* since morning.

| Future Perfect Continuous
| will + have + been + V-ing
| He *will have been studying* for 5 hours.
|===

TIP: **Perfect = have/has/had + V3** — ये formula golden है! हर perfect tense में "have" की कोई form ज़रूर है।

---

=== "Have to" vs "Must" vs "Have got to" 🤔

[cols="1,1,1"]
|===
| Have to | Must | Have got to

| External obligation
| Internal / Strong obligation
| Informal = have to

| _I **have to** wear uniform._ (rule)
| _I **must** study hard._ (self-motivated)
| _I'**ve got to** go now!_ (casual spoken)

| Past: **had to**
| No past form → use had to
| No past form → use had to

| Negative: don't have to (ज़रूरी नहीं)
| Negative: must not (मना है!)
| Negative: haven't got to

| _You **don't have to** come._ ✅
| _You **must not** come._ ❌ (opposite!)
| _You **haven't got to** worry._
|===

WARNING: **Don't have to** (ज़रूरत नहीं) ≠ **Must not** (मना है) — ये सबसे common mistake है! +
_You don't have to wear a tie._ = Tie optional है। +
_You must not wear shorts._ = Shorts मना हैं!

---

=== "Have something done" — Causative (करवाना) 🔧

जब तुम खुद काम नहीं करते, **किसी से करवाते हो** — तब Causative use होता है।

----
Subject + have/has/had + Object + V3 (Past Participle)
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मैंने अपनी car wash करवाई।
| I *had* my car *washed*.

| उसने अपने बाल कटवाए।
| She *had* her hair *cut*.

| मुझे अपना phone repair करवाना है।
| I need to *have* my phone *repaired*.

| हमने घर paint करवाया।
| We *had* the house *painted*.

| वो अपने कपड़े iron करवाता है।
| He *has* his clothes *ironed*.
|===

TIP: "करवाना" = **have + object + V3** — ये pattern daily life में बहुत use होता है!

---

=== Common Mistakes with "Have" ⚠️

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Problem क्या है

| He have a car.
| He *has* a car.
| He/She/It → has!

| She has went home.
| She has *gone* home.
| Perfect tense → V3 (gone), not V2 (went)

| I haven't a pen.
| I *don't have* a pen. / I *haven't got* a pen.
| "Haven't + noun" alone is old-fashioned — use "don't have" or "haven't got"

| Does he has a bike?
| Does he *have* a bike?
| Does के बाद base form (have), not has!

| I am having a car.
| I *have* a car.
| Possession → no continuous! Having = action (lunch, party, bath) only

| He must has studied.
| He must *have* studied.
| Modal + have (base form), not has!
|===

---

=== Tips & Tricks for "Have" 💡

* 🎯 **Have = 4 roles:**
** 1) Possession: I **have** a pen.
** 2) Perfect tenses: I **have** eaten.
** 3) Obligation: I **have to** go.
** 4) Causative: I **had** my car washed.
* 🎯 **Has = सिर्फ Present + He/She/It** — बाकी सब जगह have या had!
* 🎯 **"Having" continuous** सिर्फ action meaning में — lunch, bath, party, fun — possession में **never**!
* 🎯 **Contractions:** I've = I have, He's = He has, She'd = She had, Haven't = Have not

== 3. DO — do / does / did 🔵

"Do" सबसे simple primary auxiliary है — लेकिन सबसे ज़्यादा use होता है negative और questions बनाने में!

=== सभी Forms एक नज़र में

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Form | Tense | Example

| **do**
| Present (I/You/We/They)
| *Do* you know? I *do* yoga.

| **does**
| Present (He/She/It/Singular)
| *Does* he work? She *does* dance.

| **did**
| Past (All subjects)
| *Did* they come? I *did* try.

| **doing**
| Continuous (-ing form)
| What are you *doing*?

| **done**
| Past Participle (V3)
| The work is *done*. I have *done* it.
|===

---

=== Subject-wise कौन सी Form

[cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Subject | Present | Past

| I / You / We / They / Plural
| **do**
| **did**

| He / She / It / Singular
| **does**
| **did**
|===

NOTE: Past में सबके लिए **did** — has/does जैसा कोई confusion नहीं!

---

=== Role 1: "Do" as Main Verb (मतलब = करना) ⭐

"Do" जब main verb है तो इसका मतलब है **"करना"**।

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मैं रोज़ yoga करता हूँ।
| I *do* yoga every day.

| उसने बहुत अच्छा काम किया।
| She *did* a great job.

| तुम क्या कर रहे हो?
| What are you *doing*?

| मैंने अपना homework कर लिया।
| I have *done* my homework.

| housework करो।
| *Do* the housework.
|===

---

=== Role 2: "Do" as Auxiliary — 3 Super Powers! 🦸

"Do" का auxiliary role तीन चीज़ों के लिए है:

==== Super Power 1: Negative बनाना ❌

**Simple Present और Simple Past** में negative बनाने के लिए do/does/did + not use करते हैं।

----
Subject + do/does/did + not + V1 (base form) + Object
----

[cols="2,3,2"]
|===
| Hindi | English | Tense

| मैं coffee नहीं पीता।
| I *do not (don't)* drink coffee.
| Simple Present

| वो meat नहीं खाती।
| She *does not (doesn't)* eat meat.
| Simple Present

| मैं कल office नहीं गया।
| I *did not (didn't)* go to office yesterday.
| Simple Past

| उन्होंने call नहीं किया।
| They *did not (didn't)* call.
| Simple Past

| बारिश नहीं हुई।
| It *did not (didn't)* rain.
| Simple Past
|===

WARNING: **does/did** लगाने के बाद main verb **V1 (base form)** में रहती है! +
❌ She doesn't go**es**. → ✅ She doesn't **go**. +
❌ I didn't went. → ✅ I didn't **go**.

==== Super Power 2: Question बनाना ❓

**Simple Present और Simple Past** में question बनाने के लिए।

----
Do/Does/Did + Subject + V1 + Object + ?
----

[cols="2,3,2"]
|===
| Hindi | English | Tense

| क्या तुम चाय पीते हो?
| *Do* you drink tea?
| Simple Present

| क्या वो यहाँ काम करता है?
| *Does* he work here?
| Simple Present

| क्या तुम कल आए थे?
| *Did* you come yesterday?
| Simple Past

| उसने क्या कहा?
| What *did* she say?
| Simple Past

| तुम कहाँ रहते हो?
| Where *do* you live?
| Simple Present
|===

TIP: WH-questions pattern: **WH word + do/does/did + Subject + V1?** +
_What **do** you do? Where **does** she live? Why **did** they leave?_

==== Super Power 3: Emphasis देना 💪 (Emphatic Do)

जब ज़ोर देकर बोलना हो — "मैं **सच में** जानता हूँ!", "उसे **वाकई** पसंद है!"

----
Subject + do/does/did + V1 (emphasized)
----

[cols="2,3"]
|===
| Hindi | English

| मैं सच में जानता हूँ!
| I *do* know!

| उसे वाकई तुमसे प्यार है!
| She *does* love you!

| मैंने try तो किया था!
| I *did* try!

| Please आ जाओ!
| *Do* come! (earnest request)

| बैठो बैठो!
| *Do* sit down! (polite insistence)
|===

NOTE: Emphatic "do" normally spoken English में use होता है confirmation, insistence, या surprise express करने के लिए।

---

=== कहाँ "Do" Auxiliary Use नहीं होता ⛔

**Important Rule:** जब sentence में पहले से कोई **auxiliary verb** (is, am, are, was, were, has, have, had, will, can, should, etc.) हो, तो **do/does/did use मत करो!**

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why

| I don't am happy.
| I *am not* happy.
| "am" already auxiliary है

| Does he can swim?
| *Can* he swim?
| "can" already modal है

| She didn't has eaten.
| She *hasn't* eaten.
| "has" already auxiliary है

| Do you are coming?
| *Are* you coming?
| "are" already auxiliary है

| He didn't was there.
| He *wasn't* there.
| "was" already auxiliary है
|===

WARNING: **Golden Rule:** एक sentence में **do/does/did + another auxiliary = WRONG!** अगर पहले से be/have/modal है, तो do की ज़रूरत नहीं।

---

=== Common Mistakes with "Do" ⚠️

[cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Problem क्या है

| She doesn't goes.
| She doesn't *go*.
| does + V1 (base form), -s/-es हटाओ!

| Did he went?
| Did he *go*?
| did + V1 (base form), V2 नहीं!

| I do not am ready.
| I *am not* ready.
| am पहले से auxiliary — do मत लगाओ

| Do she likes tea?
| *Does* she like tea?
| She → does (not do) + V1 (like, not likes)

| He don't know.
| He *doesn't* know.
| He → doesn't (not don't)

| I didn't knew.
| I didn't *know*.
| did + V1!
|===

=== Tips & Tricks for "Do" 💡

* 🎯 **"Do = V-s का backup":**
** Affirmative: He go**es**. (verb पर -s)
** Negative: He **does**n't go. (does ने -s ले लिया)
** Question: **Does** he go? (does ने -s ले लिया)
** **Same with did** — did = -ed (past) ले लेता है, तो verb V1 में रहता है!
* 🎯 **कब do/does/did use करो:**
** ✅ Simple Present — negative, question
** ✅ Simple Past — negative, question
** ❌ Continuous, Perfect, Future — इनमें do नहीं!
* 🎯 **Contractions:** don't = do not, doesn't = does not, didn't = did not
* 🎯 **Emphatic Do** — ज़ोर देने के लिए: _I **do** love you! She **does** work hard!_

== Be vs Have vs Do — Comparison Table ⚔️

[cols="1,2,2,2"]
|===
| Feature | Be | Have | Do

| Forms
| am, is, are, was, were, been, being
| have, has, had
| do, does, did

| Main verb meaning
| होना (state/identity)
| पास होना (possession)
| करना (action)

| Auxiliary role
| Continuous tenses + Passive
| Perfect tenses
| Negative + Questions (Simple tenses)

| Negative बनाना
| am/is/are/was/were + **not**
| Perfect: has/have/had + **not** +V3
| do/does/did + **not** + V1

| Question बनाना
| Am/Is/Are/Was/Were + Subject?
| Has/Have/Had + Subject + V3?
| Do/Does/Did + Subject + V1?

| Continuous form?
| ✅ being (passive)
| ✅ having (action only)
| ✅ doing
|===

== Practice: खुद Translate करो! ✏️

=== Questions

1. मैं एक software engineer हूँ।
2. क्या तुम्हारे पास laptop है?
3. वो चाय नहीं पीती।
4. बच्चे park में खेल रहे थे।
5. उसने अपना काम कर लिया है।
6. क्या तुम कल office आए थे?
7. मुझे सच में उससे बात करनी चाहिए!
8. मेरे पास बहुत कम time था।
9. क्या वो यहाँ काम करता है?
10. मैंने अपनी car wash करवाई।
11. बारिश हो रही थी जब मैं office पहुँचा।
12. उसने कभी pizza नहीं खाया है।
13. तुम कहाँ हो?
14. मुझे रोज़ 6 बजे उठना पड़ता है।
15. काम हो चुका है।

=== Answers

[%collapsible]
====
1. I **am** a software engineer. _(be — identity)_
2. Do you **have** a laptop? / Have you got a laptop? _(have — possession question)_
3. She **does not (doesn't)** drink tea. _(do — negative Simple Present)_
4. The children **were** playing in the park. _(be — Past Continuous auxiliary)_
5. He **has** done his work. / He **has** finished his work. _(have — Present Perfect auxiliary)_
6. **Did** you come to the office yesterday? _(do — Simple Past question)_
7. I **do** need to talk to her! _(do — emphatic)_
8. I **had** very little time. _(have — possession, past)_
9. **Does** he work here? _(do — Simple Present question)_
10. I **had** my car washed. _(have — causative)_
11. It **was** raining when I reached the office. _(be — Past Continuous)_
12. He **has** never eaten pizza. _(have — Present Perfect)_
13. Where **are** you? _(be — location question)_
14. I **have to** wake up at 6 every day. _(have to — obligation)_
15. The work **is** done. / The work **has been** done. _(be — passive voice / have+be — perfect passive)_
====
